ARCHIVES MANAGEMENET
Foundation of Archives Administration equips students with essential knowledge and skills in records and archives management. It covers the lifecycle of records, classification techniques, preservation methods, legal frameworks, and the impact of digital technology on archiving. This subject prepares students to effectively manage and preserve both physical and digital archives, ensuring their accessibility and longevity.
CONTENT MANAGEMENT
Content management refers to the process of creating, organizing, storing, and maintaining digital content throughout its lifecycle. It involves managing various types of content, such as text, images, videos, and documents, to ensure they are easily accessible, properly organized, and up-to-date. preserve both physical and digital archives, ensuring their accessibility and longevity.
LIBRARY MANAGEMENT
Library Management involves the efficient administration and organization of library resources, services, and operations to meet the needs of users. It combines traditional methods with modern technology to manage both physical and digital resources effectively.
SYSTEM MANAGEMENT
A Basic Web Content Management System (CMS) is a software platform or application that helps users create, manage, and publish digital content on websites without needing advanced technical or coding skills. It provides a user-friendly interface for organizing content, customizing the website, and handling various functions related to web management.
